2. THE INNOVATION: UNIFIED CONTROL CENTER

Consolidating Fragmented Databases into a Single Glassmorphic HUD

The Solution (Kya)

Offline-First GIS Map & HUD

SVG-vector maps of peninsular wards showing localized flood depths. Clicks launch holographic panels detailing elevation and dispatch controls.

AI Submergence Simulator

Simulates tide heights and precipitation rates to forecast inundations. Automatically links to railway networks to suspend train routes.

Evacuation Shelters Status Board

Tracks shelter capacity and volunteer availability in real-time, matching stranded citizens to transit staging camps.

NLP Silo Parser Ingestion

Regex unifier console that parses unstructured volunteer texts (location, contacts, victim counts) and appends them to dispatch stacks instantly.

4. THE DISPATCH WORKFLOW: STEP-BY-STEP

How the Emergency Commander Coordinates Rescue Operations

Kaise (Execution)
STEP 1: INGEST

API Ingestion

Commanders review rainfall graphs and tide gauges. Railways indicators automatically detect network status alerts.

STEP 2: EXTRACT

NLP Unifier

Volunteer WhatsApp reports are processed. Structured locations, trapped counts, and contacts are extracted instantly.

STEP 3: TARGET

Doppler Scan

Commanders click GIS Wards to load local HUD overlays. Clicking 'Radar Scan' locks Doppler sweeps on coordinates.

STEP 4: ROUTE

Active Dispatch

NGO boats are dispatched with one click. Direct call buttons allow controllers to dial citizens in distress and guide them to open shelters.

5. TECHNICAL FEASIBILITY: RESILIENCE & TECH CORE

Lightweight, Offline-First Architecture Immune to Communications Blockages

Technical Stack Feasibility
[✓] React 19 & TS 5.7: Type-safe, fast rendering preventing client-side crashes on the field.
[✓] Zero External Maps: Custom SVG-vector paths mapped natively in code. Renders completely offline.
